Face Verification & Biometric Data

Critical Information: Humrah uses facial recognition technology for security purposes. Please read this section carefully to understand how your biometric data is processed.

What We Collect

When you complete video verification, we collect:

  • Short Video Recording: A brief video of your face performing liveness detection actions (e.g., turning head, blinking)
  • Face Embeddings: Mathematical representations (numerical vectors) derived from your facial features

How Face Verification Works

  1. You record a short verification video following on-screen instructions
  2. Our system analyzes the video to confirm you are a real person (liveness detection)
  3. A unique face embedding is generated from your facial features
  4. The face embedding is compared against our database to detect duplicate accounts
  5. The original video is permanently deleted within 48 hours after processing

What Are Face Embeddings?

Face embeddings are NOT images or photographs. They are:

  • Mathematical representations (arrays of numbers) that describe facial features
  • Irreversible ‚Äì they cannot be converted back into an image of your face
  • Used solely for comparing similarity between faces (duplicate detection)
  • Stored in encrypted format in secure databases

Why We Use This Technology

  • Safety: Prevents banned users from creating new accounts
  • Authenticity: Ensures all users are real people, not bots or catfish accounts
  • Trust: Builds confidence in the platform's community
  • Fraud Prevention: Detects and prevents abuse of the platform

Your Rights Regarding Biometric Data

You have the right to:

  • Know when your biometric data is being collected and why
  • Request deletion of your face embedding (by deleting your account)
  • Opt out by not using the service (face verification is mandatory for Humrah)
  • Access information about how your biometric data is stored and used

Important Notice: Face verification is mandatory to use Humrah. If you do not consent to this processing, you cannot create an account. Your face embedding will be permanently deleted when you delete your account.

Location Data

Types of Location Data

Humrah collects and uses different types of location information:

Approximate Location (Required)

  • What We Collect: City-level or neighborhood-level location
  • How: Derived from IP address or coarse location permission
  • Why: To match you with nearby companions
  • Precision: Not precise enough to identify your exact address

Precise Location (Optional)

  • What We Collect: GPS coordinates when you use location-based features
  • How: Only when you grant precise location permission
  • Why: To help you find nearby public places for meetups
  • Control: You can revoke this permission in device settings anytime

Booking Location

  • What We Collect: Public place names and addresses where meetups occur
  • Why: For booking records, dispute resolution, and safety
  • Visibility: Shared only with your matched companion

How We Use Location Data

  • Match you with nearby users within a reasonable distance
  • Suggest public places for meetups
  • Show distance estimates between you and potential companions
  • Prevent misuse by detecting unusual location patterns
  • Improve service quality in different geographic areas

Location Privacy Controls

You can control location sharing:

  • Adjust location permissions in your device settings
  • Choose to share only approximate location
  • Manually enter your city instead of using GPS
  • Turn off location services when not actively using the app

We Never: Track your location continuously in the background, share your precise location publicly, or use location data for advertising purposes.

Data Security

How We Protect Your Data

Humrah implements industry-standard security measures:

Technical Safeguards

  • Encryption: All data encrypted in transit (TLS/SSL) and at rest (AES-256)
  • Secure Storage: Databases protected by firewalls and access controls
  • Face Embeddings: Stored in encrypted format, separate from other user data
  • Authentication: OTP-based login, no passwords to be compromised
  • Regular Audits: Security reviews and penetration testing

Operational Safeguards

  • Access to user data limited to essential personnel only
  • Employees bound by confidentiality agreements
  • Multi-factor authentication for internal systems
  • Regular security training for team members
  • Incident response plan for potential breaches

Application Security

  • Regular app updates to patch vulnerabilities
  • Secure coding practices and code reviews
  • Protection against common attacks (SQL injection, XSS, CSRF)
  • Rate limiting to prevent abuse
  • Fraud detection and prevention systems

Your Role in Security

You can help protect your account:

  • Keep your device and app updated
  • Don't share OTP codes with anyone (including Humrah staff)
  • Use a secure email account
  • Report suspicious activity immediately
  • Log out from shared devices
  • Enable device-level security (PIN, biometrics)

In Case of a Breach

If a data breach occurs:

  • We will notify affected users within 72 hours

  • We will provide details about what data was affected
  • We will offer guidance on protecting yourself

No System Is 100% Secure: While we implement strong security measures, no system is completely immune to risks. You acknowledge this by using Humrah.
